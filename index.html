First-hand stories of Ukrainian farmers

Farmer Ivan

Ivan, a Ukrainian farmer who sows various agricultural crops. Year after year, he diligently cultivated the land in his plots, but this year everything changed. Mined fields have become a terrible reality for the Ukrainian people.

After the start of the military conflict, Russian soldiers invaded Ukrainian territory and mined many fields, including those belonging to Ivan. Mine after mine appeared to be planted in his native lands, turning them into dangerous and deadly zones.

Such a situation presented Ivan with a difficult choice: to give up his plot of land and lose everything he had built over the years, or to find a way to get his fields back. Many lands that were temporarily under the occupation of Russian troops are currently unfit for cultivation. And although the danger of mined fields stopped many, Ivan decided not to give up and take matters into his own hands - to independently look for organizations that can help in demining.

Otherwise, he will lose his life's work and the life of his family.

First-hand stories of Ukrainian farmers

Farmer Anton

Anton is a farmer who lived in a small village in the Kherson region. Unlike most villagers, he did not grow crops, but preferred working with animals. He ran his own animal husbandry: cows, pigs.

Anton was always someone who strived for high quality products that his farm offered to others. However, due to the large-scale invasion of Russian troops on the territory of Ukraine, Anton's farm was on the line of hostilities, and soon it was completely under occupation.

Fortunately, the animals managed to be evacuated in time and resettled on various farms in the region. After the liberation of the right bank of the Kherson region, Anton decided to return to his native land and revive his brainchild. However, during the occupation and active hostilities, the farm was damaged, and there is a possibility that the area where the cows grazed was mined by the occupiers.

In order to continue the existence of Anton's farm, he will focus on the recovery and return of his herd, it is necessary to conduct an inspection and demining of the entire area. It is impossible to do this on your own, and due to the fact that there is a huge number of similar lands throughout Ukraine, it is simply impossible to do without alternative solutions to the situation.

First-hand stories of Ukrainian farmers

Farmer Dmytro

Dmytro is a farmer from Ukraine, his whole family was engaged in growing grain and corn. Every spring, as scheduled, he began preparations for the planting season, but for the second year he has been unable to do his work because his field is still waiting for demining.

Dmytro already turned to the authorities and asked for help in demining, but the process turned out to be long and difficult. Unfortunately, the state cannot immediately solve this problem due to limited resources, as there are too many such mined areas in areas where hostilities are or have been.

In order for Dmytro's fields to be useful in the near future, alternative solutions for demining the fields are needed. Otherwise, Dmytro will be able to return to his business in at least 5 years.

First-hand stories of Ukrainian farmers

Farmer Pavlo

Farmer Pavlo was a passionate and caring owner and raised cattle on his farm. He lived in a peaceful rural area of ​​Ukraine, but after the arrival of Russian troops in the region, mines and shells remained on his pastures, which became a huge problem for Pavel and his animals.

Pavel is deeply concerned about the safety of his flock. The field where his animals grazed turned out to be mined, which posed a constant threat to their lives. He cannot simply move his animals to another place, because pastures are his only resource for livestock nutrition.

He turned to the local authorities, hoping for help in clearing his fields, but realized that the process could take a long time. All the more so now the priority is the liberation of settlements, roads and infrastructure facilities. There will not be enough employees and resources of the State Emergency Service to clear the entire land in a short period of time.

Currently, Pavlo is looking for different ways of demining the pasture on his own in order to secure his farm.
